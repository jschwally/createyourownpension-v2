The Paycheck Stops. The Bills Don't.

Most retirement strategies were built for accumulation, growing as much as possible. But retirement is a different game entirely.

When the paychecks stop, your savings have to become your income. And that's where most plans fall short.

Social Security Was Never Designed to Replace Your Full Income

It helps. But for most people it covers a fraction of what they actually spend in retirement. The gap between what Social Security pays and what you actually need is real, and it has to come from somewhere.

Market Volatility Hits Differently in Retirement

A major downturn early in retirement while you are making withdrawals can do permanent damage to your portfolio, even if markets recover later. This is called sequence of returns risk and it is one of the most overlooked threats to a secure retirement.

The 4% Rule Has a Flaw Most People Don't Know About

A significant portion of that 4% depends on market growth, not guaranteed income. Markets don't always cooperate. Especially when you need them to.

What Happens If You Do Nothing

This isn't about worst case scenarios. It's about a gap that's easy to ignore until you can't.

They Spend Less Than They Planned

Not because they want to, but because every withdrawal from a fluctuating portfolio feels uncertain. The retirement you worked decades for gets smaller because the income feels unpredictable.

They Take On More Risk Than They Should

Chasing returns to generate income late in the game. Staying aggressive when preservation matters more. One bad sequence of returns at the wrong time can be very difficult to recover from.

They Run Out of Time to Fix It

The longer you wait to create a reliable income stream, the fewer options you have and the more it costs to create the same amount of income. This is one decision where waiting has a real price.

I've Been Exactly Where You Are

30 years in financial markets taught me a lot. Losing 25% of my retirement savings twice taught me even more.
