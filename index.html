The Retirement Question Nobody Is Asking You

Your financial advisor has likely built you a retirement income plan. But is it built on guaranteed income or market withdrawals?

There is a meaningful difference between the two and most people never think to ask. A withdrawal strategy depends on markets cooperating. A guaranteed income plan pays you regardless of what markets do. During your working years your paycheck arrived on schedule no matter what the market did. In retirement your income should work the same way.

The market gets a vote on when you retire

Your retirement date should not depend on whether the market is up or down the year you plan to stop working. But if your income depends entirely on your portfolio, the market gets a vote on when you retire. One poor sequence of returns at the wrong time can force you to work longer than you planned or spend less than you hoped.

Social Security was not designed to carry the load

It helps. But for most people it covers a fraction of what they actually spend. The gap between what Social Security pays and what you actually need has to come from somewhere.

Your financial advisor may not be your income specialist

There is a difference between an advisor who grows your money and one who knows how to turn it into income. Most people never think to ask which one they have.

The good news: there is a straightforward solution.

And finding it starts with one number.

What an Income Plan Actually Looks Like

It is simpler than you think and it does not mean giving up your portfolio.

An income plan starts with one question: what does it cost to live your life in retirement every month?

Housing. Healthcare. Food. Transportation. The bills that show up whether the market is up or down. We call these your essential expenses and they are the foundation of everything.

Once you know that number, you compare it to your guaranteed income sources. Social Security. Any pension. Anything else that arrives on schedule regardless of market conditions.

The difference between what you need and what you have guaranteed is your income gap. That gap is what we are solving for.

Illustrative example only. Your numbers will vary.

Here is where most people stop.

They assume the gap has to be filled by their portfolio. By withdrawals. By hoping the market cooperates. But there is another way.

A portion of your retirement savings can be converted into a Pension Strategy, a guaranteed monthly income stream that covers your essential expenses for life, regardless of how long you live or what the market does. Not all of your savings. Just enough to close the gap.

You Are in Control

Unlike Social Security, your Pension Strategy income cannot be reduced by Congress, cannot be delayed by a change in retirement age, and is not subject to political decisions you have no control over. You decide when income starts. You lock in the amount. The insurance carrier is contractually obligated to pay it for life.

Social Security is your baseline income. Your Pension Strategy builds on top of it. Together they create an income foundation that no market downturn, no political decision, and no change in retirement rules can take away.

What that does to the rest of your portfolio is remarkable. It no longer has to carry the full burden of your lifestyle. It can stay invested. It can recover from downturns. It can grow and be used for the things that make retirement genuinely enjoyable.

Travel. Family. Home. The causes you care about.

A Tale of Two Strategies

Meet Hope and Helen

Same age. Same savings. Very different retirement outcomes.

Hope and Helen are twin sisters. Both turned 60 with $500,000 saved for retirement. Both wanted $40,000 a year in retirement income starting at age 70. Both experienced the same market returns over the same 25 years.

The only difference was their strategy.

Hope kept her full $500,000 in the market, let it grow for 10 years to $1,000,000, then started withdrawing $40,000 a year. A reasonable plan that most people would feel good about.

Helen moved a portion of her savings into a Pension Strategy at age 60, left the rest in the market, and locked in $40,000 a year in guaranteed income starting at age 70. Her portfolio never needed to fund a single dollar of her income.

By age 94, after both sisters had taken $1,000,000 in income over 25 years, the results were remarkably different.

Helen came out ahead by $1,481,146. With the same income. From the same starting amount. In the same market.

The difference was not luck. It was a Pension Strategy.
