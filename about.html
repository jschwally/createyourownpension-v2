Our Story

30 Years in Financial Markets Taught Me a Lot. Losing 25% of My Retirement Savings Twice Taught Me Even More.

I am not here to manage your portfolio. I am here to help you create income you can never outlive.

Why I Do This Work

I spent more than 30 years in financial services, working at investment banks, broker dealers, and insurance firms. I held FINRA Series 7 and Series 66 licenses, a NASDAQ trading license, and worked across virtually every corner of the financial markets. For most of that career I was all in on accumulation, chasing returns and maximizing growth. It worked. Until it didn't.

I lost more than 25% of my retirement savings during the Dot Com collapse between 2000 and 2002. Then I watched it happen again during the Great Financial Crisis in 2007 and 2008. Those experiences changed how I think about money in retirement completely. As retirement got closer, I stopped asking how much I could make and started asking how much income I could create. Aleece and I made a decision together. We traded a portion of our retirement savings for a guaranteed income stream. Not all of it. Just enough to cover our essential living expenses every month, guaranteed, regardless of what markets do. That decision changed everything. It took pressure off our portfolio and stress off our lives. And it is the same decision I now help other Texans think through every day.

Experience You Can Count On

I was born in New York, raised in Florida, and spent years living across the country before Texas became home. And home it has been for 30 years. I would not have it any other way. My career has always been rooted in financial services and sales. Over three decades I worked at investment banks, broker dealers, and insurance firms, holding securities licenses that gave me an inside view of some of the most complex corners of the financial markets. Those licenses are now inactive because my focus is no longer on managing portfolios or chasing returns. It is on one thing: helping people create guaranteed lifetime income.

I hold an active Texas Life and Health Insurance license, which is the credential that matters for the work I do today. I also hold a Texas real estate sales license and help friends and family in the DFW area when the opportunity arises. What I bring to every client conversation is not just credentials. It is 30 years of watching financial markets from the inside, and the personal experience of navigating two major market collapses on my own retirement savings.

What Working With Me Looks Like

Simple, transparent, and always at your pace.

1

The Discovery Call

We start with a conversation. No paperwork, no pressure, no sales pitch. I want to understand your retirement picture, what you have, what you spend, what keeps you up at night, and what you are hoping retirement looks like. This call is about you, not about products.

2

The Income Gap Analysis

Using the information you share, we map out your retirement income picture. Guaranteed income sources on one side, essential expenses on the other. The gap between them is what we are solving for. I will bring up expenses or income sources you may not have considered. But only you can define what is essential in your life.

3

Exploring Your Options

If guaranteed income makes sense for your situation, I will show you specifically what it could look like. Real numbers from real carriers based on your age, your timeline, and the amount you are comfortable allocating. If it does not make sense, I will tell you that too.

4

The Decision Is Yours

How much of your gap to cover with guaranteed income, or whether to cover any of it, is entirely your decision. It depends on your overall financial picture, your confidence in your plan, your risk tolerance, and factors unique to your life. My role is to guide you through that decision, not make it for you.

5

Implementation

If you decide to move forward, I handle the process from application to issue. I work with multiple carriers to find the product that best fits your specific situation. And I stay available after implementation because this is a long term relationship, not a transaction.
