My Story

I spent more than 30 years in financial services. Investment banks, broker dealers, insurance firms. I held FINRA Series 7 and Series 66 securities licenses, a NASDAQ trading license, and worked across virtually every corner of the financial markets. For most of that career I was all in on accumulation, chasing returns and maximizing growth.

It worked. Until it didn't.

I lost more than 25% of my retirement savings during the Dot Com collapse between 2000 and 2002. Then I watched it happen again during the Great Financial Crisis in 2007 and 2008. Two major gut punches that most people only read about in headlines. I lived them and I felt every one of them.

Those experiences changed how I think about money in retirement completely.

As retirement got closer I stopped asking how much I could make and started asking how much income I could create. Aleece and I made a decision together. We traded a portion of our retirement savings for a guaranteed income stream. Not all of it. Just enough to cover our essential living expenses every month, guaranteed, regardless of what markets do.

That decision changed everything. It took pressure off our portfolio and stress off our lives. And it is the same decision I now help other Texans think through every day.

My Retirement Philosophy

During your working years income is simple. You earn a paycheck. You spend it. You save what is left.

But retirement changes that completely. The paycheck stops and now your income must be created from everything you have built.

Many retirement strategies rely heavily on withdrawals from an investment portfolio. The 4% rule sounds reasonable on the surface. But a significant portion of that 4% depends on market growth, not guaranteed income. In fact, the average dividend yield of the S&P 500 has been approximately 1.84% over the last 25 years, meaning more than half of that 4% withdrawal has to come from market appreciation rather than actual income.

You are relying on markets to cooperate. And markets do not always cooperate, especially when you need them to.

My philosophy is straightforward. Take market timing out of the equation for the income you cannot afford to lose. Use a portion of your retirement savings to create a reliable income stream that covers your essential expenses. Not all of your assets. Just enough.

Once your essential expenses are covered by dependable income something important happens. Your portfolio no longer has to carry the full burden of your lifestyle. It can stay invested. It can recover from downturns. It can be positioned for long term growth.

Cover what must be covered. Let the rest of your portfolio do what it does best.
