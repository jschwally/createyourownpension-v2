Questions and Disclosures

Everything you need to know before we talk.

Getting Started

What exactly do you do?

I am a retirement income specialist. My focus is one thing: helping people create a guaranteed stream of income they cannot outlive. I do not manage investment portfolios, offer tax advice, or provide legal counsel. If you need those services, I am happy to point you toward people who can help.

Who do you typically work with?

I work with individuals and couples who are either approaching retirement or already retired, typically between the ages of 55 and 75. Most of my clients have at least $500,000 saved for retirement and are looking to create a reliable income floor to cover their essential living expenses.

Do I have to move all my investments over to you?

No, and I would not want you to. I am not an investment advisor and I do not manage portfolios. Most clients keep their current financial advisor and simply add a guaranteed income strategy alongside their existing investments. If you are looking for an investment advisor or thinking about making a change, I am happy to suggest a few good ones.

What licenses do you hold?

I have been a licensed insurance agent in Texas since 2018, authorized to sell Life, Accident, Health, and HMO products. License number 2266812. Annuities are life insurance products which falls within that license. I also hold an active Texas real estate sales license. I previously held FINRA Series 7 and Series 66 securities licenses and a NASDAQ trading license, but those are now inactive since I no longer manage investments.

Are you a fiduciary?

Insurance agents are held to a suitability standard of care, which means recommendations must be appropriate based on your individual situation. The fiduciary standard is a separate legal designation that applies to Registered Investment Advisors. That said, nothing prevents me from operating with a fiduciary mindset, and that is exactly how I approach every client relationship. I want to do right by you because it is the right thing to do.

The Process

How does the process work?

We start with a conversation to understand your retirement income picture, what you have coming in, what you spend, and what gap exists between the two. From there I present potential solutions from multiple carriers based on your specific situation. You decide what makes sense, and I handle everything from application through implementation. We check in annually and can adjust your income timing if your situation or goals change. The decision is always yours. I am here to guide, not to tell you what to do.

How long does the process take?

From our first conversation to a policy being placed, the typical timeline is two to four weeks depending on the source of funds, the carrier, and the complexity of your situation. There is no rush. We move at your pace.

The Product

What carriers do you work with?

I work with approximately 40 highly rated insurance carriers rather than representing a single company. That independence allows me to find the product that best fits your specific situation rather than fitting your situation to a product I am required to sell.

What is a Fixed Indexed Annuity and how is it different from other annuities?

A Fixed Indexed Annuity, or FIA, is an insurance product that offers the potential for growth tied to a market index without direct market exposure. Your principal is protected from market losses. The specific feature we focus on is called an income rider, which we refer to as a Pension Strategy. It creates a guaranteed income stream you cannot outlive regardless of market conditions or how long you live. This is different from a variable annuity which has direct market exposure, and from an immediate annuity which converts a lump sum to income right away with no further accumulation.

How much guaranteed income will I receive?

Your income amount is determined before the application is submitted, based on the carrier, the specific product and riders selected, the premium amount, whether coverage is for a single life or joint life, and when you want income to begin. You will know that number before you commit to anything. Keep in mind that any change to your income start date will affect the income amount.

Is there a limit to how much I can put into an annuity?

Yes. Insurance carriers generally will not allow you to put more than half of your liquid net worth into any single annuity. In practice, Create Your Own Pension stays well below that threshold because we are only solving for your essential expenses, not your entire financial picture.

Costs and Taxes

Can I access my money if I need it in an emergency?

Most FIA policies allow you to withdraw up to 10% of your account value each year without surrender charges. Withdrawals beyond that during the surrender period may be subject to charges that vary by carrier and product. We will walk through the specific liquidity provisions of any product before you make a decision.

What happens to my money if I die before I use all of it?

Most policies include a death benefit that passes the remaining account value to your named beneficiaries. The specifics depend on the carrier and the product selected. We will cover this clearly before any application is submitted.

How are you paid and what will this cost me?

You never write a separate check for my services. The insurance carrier pays me a one-time commission after your policy is placed, ranging from 1% to 7% of the premium depending on the product. Your account value on day one equals what you put in. If you deposit $100,000 your account value on day one is $100,000. The carrier charges an annual fee for the income rider, typically between 1% and 1.5% of the benefit base, deducted from your account value until income begins. All costs are disclosed clearly before you make any decision.

How is my annuity income taxed?

Annuities offer tax-deferred growth, meaning you won't owe taxes on your funds while they grow. However, when you begin to take income or withdraw funds, those payments will be subject to taxation depending on whether you have a qualified or non-qualified annuity. If you purchase with qualified money such as an IRA or 401k, the full income payment is generally taxable as ordinary income. If you use after-tax dollars, only the portion representing growth on your original premium is typically subject to tax. We strongly recommend consulting your tax advisor to determine the approach that makes the most sense for your situation.

Protection and Guarantees

How is my annuity protected if something happens to the insurance company?

Every insurance company doing business in Texas is required to be a member of the Texas Life and Health Insurance Guaranty Association, or TLHIGA. TLHIGA provides certain protections for policyholders if a member company becomes insolvent, up to coverage limits that vary by policy type. It is important to understand that TLHIGA is an industry funded safety net, not a government guarantee, and should not be the primary basis for selecting a carrier. Evaluating carrier financial strength remains an important part of the process and something we address together. For complete details see the TLHIGA disclosure in the General Disclosures section below, or visit www.txlifega.org.

What about inflation, rising costs, or economic downturns? Will those affect my income?

No. External factors such as inflation, economic conditions, unemployment, or stock market performance do not alter your guaranteed annuity income. That is the entire point of the strategy. Your income is contractually guaranteed by the insurance carrier regardless of what is happening in the broader economy. Your market-based investments may be affected by those factors, but your income floor will not be.
