Pension Strategy · Illustration

Same $40,000 a Year. Very Different Retirements.

Hope and Helen both retire at 65. Both take $40,000 a year in retirement income for the next 25 years, the same total, the same 25 years, even the same market. The only difference is where that income comes from, and it changes everything about what's left over at the end.

Hope
Draws income from her portfolio
Retires at age 65 with a $1,000,000 portfolio
Withdraws $40,000 a year directly from that portfolio
Her balance rides the market up and down every year
Helen
Draws income from a Pension Strategy
Retires at age 65 with a $500,000 portfolio
Set up her Pension Strategy years earlier to pay her $40,000 a year, guaranteed
Her portfolio is never touched to generate that income

Both took home the exact same $1,000,000 in total income. $0 in excess portfolio growth for Helen, simply because of where her income came from
What this doesn't show: these numbers are flat, they don't increase for inflation, and they don't account for portfolio management fees, both of which would further reduce Hope's ending balance. This illustration also doesn't include what it cost Helen to set up her Pension Strategy years earlier, that's part of the conversation worth having on a call.
